## East Region

### 1. Duke Blue Devils
* **Key Player:** Cam Boozer (Freshman)
* **Summary:** Duke enters as arguably the best team in the country after winning the ACC regular season and tournament. They are anchored by the #1 defense in KenPom and a generational star in Boozer.
* **Strengths:** Elite post-scoring and interior presence.
Top-ranked defensive efficiency.
* **Weaknesses:** History of blowing late leads (Texas Tech/UNC losses).
Prone to letting opponents climb back into games.

### 2. UConn Huskies
* **Key Player:** Alex Karaban
* **Summary:** Despite a down year for the Big East, UConn remains a defensive stalwart with plenty of veteran leadership. However, their offense has been prone to long dry spells that led to surprising conference losses.
* **Strengths:** Veteran tournament experience (Karaban/Solo Ball).
High-level defensive floor.
* **Weaknesses:** Offensive stagnation and scoring ruts.
Lack of overwhelming physical bigs compared to previous years.

### 3. Michigan State Spartans
* **Key Player:** Jeremy Fears
* **Summary:** Tom Izzo has a carbon copy of last year's Elite 8 squad, relying on grit and fundamental rebounding. While they lack a one-and-done superstar, they are dangerous on neutral courts.
* **Strengths:** Top 5 in both offensive and defensive rebounding efficiency.
Elite coaching in March settings.
* **Weaknesses:** Lack of a generational scoring talent.
Lower offensive ceiling in high-scoring games.

### 4. Kansas Jayhawks
* **Key Player:** Darren Peterson
* **Summary:** Kansas looked like a juggernaut in mid-winter but enters the tournament cold and out of rhythm. The Darren Peterson saga has left the team's chemistry in question heading into the first round.
* **Strengths:** High-end talent and coaching pedigree.
Proven ability to beat elite teams (Arizona/BYU).
* **Weaknesses:** Entering the tournament with zero momentum.
Lack of rhythm for star player Darren Peterson.

### 5. St. John's Red Storm
* **Key Player:** Zuby Ejiofor
* **Summary:** Rick Pitino has the Johnnies playing their best basketball at the perfect time after winning the Big East tournament. They have transitioned from a post-heavy team to one with a surging backcourt.
* **Strengths:** Zuby Ejiofor's dominance as the Big East's best player.
Elite momentum (only 1 loss since mid-season).
* **Weaknesses:** Early season inconsistency (started 9-5).
Questions about the Bryce Hopkins/Ejiofor spacing.

### 6. Louisville Cardinals
* **Key Player:** Michael Brown Jr. (Freshman)
* **Summary:** Louisville returns to the dance with an elite freshman guard who can take over games single-handedly. They are an experienced group that thrives when their backcourt is hitting shots.
* **Strengths:** Elite individual playmaking from Michael Brown Jr.
Veteran experience across the roster.
* **Weaknesses:** Inability to win big games against top-tier opponents this year.
High reliance on freshman scoring.

### 7. UCLA Bruins
* **Key Player:** Donovan Dent
* **Summary:** Mick Cronin's squad survived a rollercoaster season by finding their defensive identity late. They play an annoying slow-paced style that can frustrate higher-seeded teams.
* **Strengths:** Elite guard play from New Mexico transfer Donovan Dent.
Slow tempo that limits possessions for favorites.
* **Weaknesses:** Limited offensive explosiveness.
Struggles to keep up if they fall behind early.

### 8. Ohio State Buckeyes
* **Key Player:** Bruce Thornton
* **Summary:** Under Jake Diebler, the Buckeyes finally got star Bruce Thornton into the tournament. They are a resilient group that rarely loses back-to-back games.
* **Strengths:** Elite veteran leadership in Thornton.
Consistent performance (rarely suffers bad losses).
* **Weaknesses:** Lack of depth behind the primary three scorers.
Defensive lapses against high-tempo teams.

### 9. TCU Horned Frogs
* **Key Player:** Jamie Dixon's scrappy veterans
* **Summary:** TCU is a physical, hand-checking team that plays a gritty style under Jamie Dixon. They are the ultimate wildcard capable of upsetting a 1-seed or losing to a mid-major.
* **Strengths:** Physicality and forced turnovers.
High-level non-conference wins (Florida/Wisconsin).
* **Weaknesses:** Terrible losses to low-tier opponents (New Orleans/Notre Dame).
Inconsistent offensive production.

### 10. UCF Knights
* **Key Player:** Johnny Dawkins' veteran core
* **Summary:** The Knights survived the brutal Big 12 gauntlet but enter March on a downward trend. They struggled significantly against the bottom half of their conference late in the year.
* **Strengths:** Experience playing against elite competition daily.
Defensive grit.
* **Weaknesses:** Poor momentum (multiple 3-game losing streaks).
Struggles against mid-major athleticism.

### 11. South Florida Bulls
* **Key Player:** Brian Hodgson's transfer core
* **Summary:** One of the hottest teams in the country, USF hasn't lost a game since January. They play at a breakneck speed and dominate the boards despite their size.
* **Strengths:** Third longest win streak in the nation.
Top 7 rebounding team in KenPom.
* **Weaknesses:** Roster primarily made of new transfers (chemistry risks).
History of funky games against slower opponents.

### 12. Northern Iowa Panthers
* **Key Player:** Ben Jacobson's veterans
* **Summary:** UNI is an analytical darling that plays at the second-slowest tempo in the country. They are an elite defensive unit that won four games in four days to steal a bid.
* **Strengths:** #5 in the nation in minute continuity.
Slow tempo that neutralizes athletic advantages.
* **Weaknesses:** Limited scoring options if forced to play fast.
Struggles when trailing late due to slow pace.

### 13. CA Baptist Lancers
* **Key Player:** WAC Championship core
* **Summary:** In just their seventh season of D1 play, the Lancers have made their first-ever tournament. They are a high-level rebounding team that plays with house money.
* **Strengths:** Elite rebounding for a mid-major.
Nothing to lose as a first-time participant.
* **Weaknesses:** Lack of tournament-level experience.
Poor shooting numbers against Power Five defenses.

### 14. North Dakota St. Bison
* **Key Player:** Damari Wheeler-Thomas
* **Summary:** A staple of the tournament under Dave Richmond, the Bison are back as a disciplined but undersized unit. They rely on Wheeler-Thomas to provide bucket-getter magic.
* **Strengths:** Tournament-tested coaching (Dave Richmond).
Elite individual scoring from Wheeler-Thomas.
* **Weaknesses:** One of the smallest teams in the field.
Lack of athletes to contend with high-major wings.

### 15. Furman Paladins
* **Key Player:** Alex Wilkins (Freshman)
* **Summary:** Furman is a tall, dangerous 15-seed that only recently got healthy. They have a history of 15-over-2 upsets and feature a freshman guard who is a pure bucket.
* **Strengths:** Elite height/length for a mid-major.
Tournament-proven giant killer pedigree.
* **Weaknesses:** Inconsistent regular season due to injuries.
High turnover rate when pressured.

### 16. Siena Saints
* **Key Player:** Gavin Doty
* **Summary:** Barry McNamara has revitalized Siena, taking them from the bottom of the MAAC to the Big Dance in two years. They are a great story but face a massive talent gap.
* **Strengths:** Excellent head coaching and quick turnaround momentum.
Passionate fan base and Beloo the mascot.
* **Weaknesses:** Massive talent gap vs high-major seeds.
Poor interior defense.

## South Region

### 1. Florida Gators
* **Key Player:** Micah Handlogten / Alex Condon
* **Summary:** The reigning champs survived a 5-4 start to become a rebounding juggernaut. Their frontcourt is arguably the best in the country, playing a style reminiscent of old Roy Williams teams.
* **Strengths:** Top 10 rebounding on both ends.
The most physical frontcourt in the field.
* **Weaknesses:** Inconsistent backcourt play (Xavian Lee/Boogie Fland).
Lacks the clutch playmaking of last year's roster.

### 2. Houston Cougars
* **Key Player:** Kingston Flemings (Freshman)
* **Summary:** Houston remains a defensive powerhouse that forces turnovers and plays at a snail's pace. While efficient, there are concerns about their offensive ceiling in a high-stakes environment.
* **Strengths:** Elite at forcing turnovers and protecting the ball.
Perfect mix of freshman talent and veteran leadership.
* **Weaknesses:** Offense frequently gets stuck in half-court sets.
Lacks a true takeover offensive factor.

### 3. Illinois Fighting Illini
* **Key Player:** Katon Waggler (Freshman)
* **Summary:** Illinois boasts the #1 offense on KenPom and is the tallest team in college basketball. The emergence of Waggler has transformed them into a legitimate Final Four contender.
* **Strengths:** #1 KenPom offensive efficiency.
Unmatched height and length at every position.
* **Weaknesses:** Struggles in close games (4 overtime losses).
Defensive lapses against quick, small guards.

### 4. Nebraska Cornhuskers
* **Key Player:** Sam Hoiberg / Rink Mast
* **Summary:** This is the best Nebraska team in history, looking for their first-ever tournament win. They are a veteran-led, elite defensive group that runs clinical offensive sets.
* **Strengths:** Elite defensive discipline and transition tracking.
High-level veteran leadership (Mast/Hoiberg).
* **Weaknesses:** The mental hurdle of never having won a tourney game.
Prone to playing to the level of inferior opponents.

### 5. Vanderbilt Commodores
* **Key Player:** Duke Miles
* **Summary:** Vanderbilt is a fun, backcourt-oriented team that protects the ball and hits free throws. They proved their toughness by beating physical teams like Tennessee and Florida late in the year.
* **Strengths:** Elite free-throw shooting and ball security.
Dynamic guard play (Duke Miles/Tyler Tanner).
* **Weaknesses:** Vulnerability against high-major physical interior play.
Defensive rebounding struggles.

### 6. North Carolina Tar Heels
* **Key Player:** Seth Trimble
* **Summary:** The Heels are bigger and older than last year, but the loss of star Caleb Wilson is a massive blow. They fixed their post-presence issues but remain vulnerable to elite shooting teams.
* **Strengths:** Improved interior defense and size.
Veteran experience under Hubert Davis.
* **Weaknesses:** Best player (Caleb Wilson) is out with an injury.
Poor perimeter defense against guard-heavy teams.

### 7. Saint Mary's Gaels
* **Key Player:** Randy Bennett's offensive system
* **Summary:** The Gaels are the same as always-slow, disciplined, and efficient. This year's version is a much better shooting team, ranking top 15 in 3-point percentage.
* **Strengths:** #1 in the nation in free-throw shooting percentage.
Top 15 in 3-point accuracy.
* **Weaknesses:** Struggles against high-level athleticism.
Vulnerable if forced into a high-possession game.

### 10. Texas A&M Aggies (Bucky Ball)
* **Key Player:** Bucky McMillan's transfer core
* **Summary:** Bucky Ball took the SEC by storm with high-pressure defense and relentless energy. However, they lost steam late, and the league seemed to have figured out their chaotic style.
* **Strengths:** High-pressure defense that shocks opponents.
Relentless effort and depth.
* **Weaknesses:** Poor momentum (bad loss in SEC tourney).
Offensive reliance on low-efficiency shots.

### 11. VCU Rams
* **Key Player:** Terrence Hill
* **Summary:** VCU continues to be an A10 powerhouse regardless of who is coaching. They found their identity late and have enough offensive firepower to pull off a first-round upset.
* **Strengths:** Postseason pedigree and A10 Power status.
Strong offensive rhythm from Terrence Hill.
* **Weaknesses:** Early season identity crisis (started 6-4).
Roster lack of height compared to high-major seeds.

### 12. McNeese State Cowboys
* **Key Player:** Javon Garcia
* **Summary:** Riding a 10-game win streak, McNeese is a KenPom top-60 team for the third straight year. They are the ultimate trap team for any power-conference opponent that plays sloppy.
* **Strengths:** Relentless turnover-forcing defense.
High-level confidence from a 10-game win streak.
* **Weaknesses:** Vulnerability against high-major physical size.
Over-reliance on forcing turnovers to score.

### 13. Troy Trojans
* **Key Player:** Victor Valdez / Thomas Dow
* **Summary:** Troy has been a mid-major darling since November, with wins over San Diego State and Akron. They have a dominant frontcourt duo that can punish teams with weak interiors.
* **Strengths:** Proven giant-killer wins (SDS/USC/Akron).
Dominant Valdez/Dow frontcourt duo.
* **Weaknesses:** Lack of depth behind their star duo.
Prone to cold shooting stretches from the perimeter.

### 14. Penn Quakers
* **Key Player:** TJ Power
* **Summary:** Fran McCaffery has Penn back in the dance behind Duke/Virginia transfer TJ Power. They are a dangerous Ivy League team that lives and dies by the three.
* **Strengths:** TJ Power's high-major scoring ability.
Disciplined Ivy League offensive sets.
* **Weaknesses:** Defensive struggles against athletic wings.
Recent history of close games that could go either way.

### 15. Idaho Vandals
* **Key Player:** Brody Robbery
* **Summary:** Idaho won four games in five days to make their first tourney since 1990. They have a Peak Gonzaga look to them and a brand-new arena that has revitalized the program.
* **Strengths:** Brody Robbery's interior scoring.
Insane momentum after a Starch Madness run.
* **Weaknesses:** Massive step up in competition.
One-dimensional offensive attack.

### 16. Prairie View A&M / Lehigh (First Four)
* **Key Player:** First Four winner
* **Summary:** PVAMU won as an 8-seed in the SWAC, while Lehigh returns for the first time since their 2012 upset of Duke. Both are likely destined for the First Four in Dayton.
* **Strengths:** High-pressure defense (PVAMU).
Tenured coaching (Lehigh).
* **Weaknesses:** Turnover prone.
Undersized against top seeds.

## West Region

* **Key Player:** Jaden Bradley / Braden Burries

* **Strengths:** Elite transition basketball and team chemistry.
High-major wins over Houston, UConn, and Florida.
* **Weaknesses:** Historically knocking on the door but failing to reach the Final Four.
Vulnerable if Bradley/Burries are both in foul trouble.

### 2. Purdue Boilermakers

Strong veteran leadership in the backcourt.
* **Weaknesses:** Uncharacteristic regular-season home struggles.
Inconsistent production from Tofman Ren.

### 3. Gonzaga Bulldogs

Freshmen (Supree/Davis) stepping up late.

Struggles against elite, athletic high-major teams.

Elite comeback capability.

## Midwest Region

### 1. Michigan Wolverines
* **Key Player:** Yakel Lindborg / De Mara
* **Summary:** Michigan finished the season as the #1 defense on KenPom. They are an elite physical team, but they have a tendency to let teams hang around, which could be fatal in March.
* **Strengths:** #1 KenPom defensive efficiency.
Dominant post presence (De Mara).
* **Weaknesses:** Offensive droughts if Lindborg is contained.
Loss of emerging guard LJ Kase.

### 2. Iowa State Cyclones
* **Key Player:** Tamin Lipsey / Milan Momcilovic

* **Strengths:** Elite 3-point shooting (Momcilovic).
Veteran leadership in Lipsey.
* **Weaknesses:** Past history of early-round exits.
Perception of being a home-court only team.

### 3. Virginia Cavaliers
* **Key Player:** Chance Malloy / Tess Ditter

Freshman stars (Malloy/Ditter) providing a spark.

Lack of a true high-major signature win.

### 4. Alabama Crimson Tide

Dynamic backcourt (Phylon is a Kemba candidate).

Inconsistent defensive effort.

### 5. Texas Tech Red Raiders

Lack of a second-weekend it factor.

### 7. Kentucky Wildcats
